Executive Summary

AirBed&Breakfast (2008) presents a compelling marketplace thesis with a clear value proposition on both sides — travelers save money, hosts make money. The team is strong on product (2 RISD designers + Harvard CS engineer) with a clever adoption strategy targeting events where hotels sell out. Where the deck falls short is operational and financial depth: no burn rate, no use-of-funds breakdown, no discussion of trust/safety infrastructure, and no growth metrics. The PG Thinking score (16/20) tells the real story — this is a team with genuine hustle, contrarian thinking, and unscalable early tactics that signal founder quality. The financial thinness is typical of a 2008 angel deck and shouldn't overshadow the strength of the insight.

Missing Information
  • F2 · F3 · P3 No burn rate or use-of-funds breakdown. $500K ask with no allocation detail.
  • P2 Rate limiter not addressed. Supply-side (getting hosts to list) is the obvious bottleneck for a marketplace — how do you solve cold start?
  • P8 · CC Trust and safety not discussed. Strangers staying in strangers' homes requires identity verification, payment escrow, damage protection, and dispute resolution.
  • F7 · F8 No margin or cash flow analysis. 10% take rate on $80/night bookings is thin — what are the payment processing, support, and infrastructure costs?
  • PG13 No growth metrics. How many bookings this month vs. last? What's the week-over-week trajectory?
