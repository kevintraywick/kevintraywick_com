Tier 1 — Must Have
F1Revenue ModelPresent
Clear robots-as-a-service model: farmers pay per-acre service fees for the robots to weed their fields. No upfront robot purchase — the company retains ownership, deploys, and operates the fleet. Simple, recurring model appropriate for the customer base.
F2Monthly Burn RatePartial
~$150K/month burn inferred from third-party reporting (Kingscrowd analysis of SEC crowdfunding filings) not stated directly by the company. No breakdown of cost components is publicly available. Partial credit for the number being traceable to audited financials.
F3Use of FundsPartial
General categories mentioned (fleet expansion, operations) in crowdfunding materials but no itemized dollar allocation. An investor cannot verify whether the raise covers the stated period of growth without specific line items.
F4Runway CalculationAbsent
Not stated. With ~$150K/month burn and minimal revenue, runway is acutely short but the company does not calculate or disclose how many months the current raise covers.
Tier 2 — Should Have
F53-Year P&L ProjectionAbsent
No forward-looking financial model is publicly available. Revenue grew 32% YoY ($148K → $196K) but no projection of when the fleet size, service fees, and margins converge to profitability.
F6Unit Economics (CAC/LTV)Partial
Gross margin of ~7% is known from SEC filings, indicating the per-robot cost of service is very close to the service fee charged. No explicit CAC or LTV data. The margin trajectory — how 7% becomes viable as the fleet scales — is not shown.
F7Gross Margin TrajectoryPartial
Current gross margin (~7%) is derivable from public filings. The argument for improvement at scale — lower robot manufacturing cost, higher utilization, automation of field supervision — is reasonable but not documented with a model.
F812-Month Cash Flow ProjectionAbsent
Not available publicly. Monthly cash flow is critical here because the farming season creates lumpy revenue — robots work spring through fall, creating seasonal burn peaks that need to be modeled explicitly.
Tier 3 — Nice to Have
F9Cap Table SummaryPartial
Strategic investors (Chipotle, MKC, ILS) are named and the company has raised $35M total through multiple rounds including public equity crowdfunding. Exact ownership percentages and dilution schedule not disclosed.
F10Revenue AssumptionsAbsent
No bottom-up revenue model: how many acres per robot per season, at what service fee per acre, with what robot utilization rate. The math connecting fleet size to revenue is not shown.
F11Sensitivity AnalysisAbsent
Not present. Particularly important here because weather, crop yields, and herbicide price are all variables that affect farmer willingness to pay for robotic weeding.
F12Comparable BenchmarksAbsent
No financial benchmarking against FarmWise (wound down), Carbon Robotics (laser model, different economics), or traditional herbicide cost per acre. Without comps, the $40M valuation on $196K revenue is hard to contextualize.
