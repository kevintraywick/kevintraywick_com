Executive Summary

Greenfield Robotics is a genuinely mission-driven company solving a real and growing problem — herbicide resistance and chemical dependency in broadacre farming — with a working fleet of 20 robots that pay farmers are actively using. Founder Clint Brauer scores exceptionally well on PG dimensions: personal origin story, domain obsession, schlep tolerance, and relentless resourcefulness in avoiding conventional VC. The core weakness is financial: after seven-plus years and $35M raised, revenue sits at $196K annually with a 7% gross margin and ~$150K/month burn. The business model has not yet proven that it can scale to positive unit economics, and the public materials lack the financial depth — runway, use of funds, projections — that even an early-stage investor needs to have an informed conversation.

Missing Information
  • F2 · F3 · F4 · P3 No explicit burn rate breakdown, use-of-funds allocation, or runway calculation in public materials. ~$150K/month burn is inferred from media coverage, not stated by the company.
  • P2 · P7 Rate limiter to $10M revenue is not addressed — robot manufacturing capacity, field operations scaling, and per-robot economics need to be reconciled. At $196K revenue and 7% gross margin, the path to $10M is not visible.
  • F5 · F8 · F10 No 3-year P&L, cash flow model, or revenue assumption documentation. Investors cannot assess how many robots at what price per acre gets the company to profitability.
  • P5 · P8 Survival runway without new funding is a critical red flag — with current burn exceeding revenue by 100x, the company is entirely dependent on continued external capital. Single point of failure is not articulated.
  • P6 First critical hire not identified. At this stage, a CFO or VP of Operations capable of managing a hardware-as-a-service fleet at scale is likely the blocking hire.
