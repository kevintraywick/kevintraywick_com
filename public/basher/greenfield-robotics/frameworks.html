Bill Payne · Scorecard
Strong mission, thin commercial infrastructure
The market opportunity scores high — herbicide resistance is a $30B+ problem growing in urgency. The technology works (20 deployed robots, multiple seasons). But the team lacks commercial depth: no CFO, no VP Sales, no operational scaling experience. Marketing channels rely on crowdfunding and farmer word-of-mouth rather than a structured go-to-market. The funding need is acute with burn far exceeding revenue. Composite: ~99% — just below the C threshold.

Dave Berkus · Risk
Idea proven, product working, business model unvalidated at scale
Sound idea earns full marks — chemical-free farming is not a trend, it's a structural shift. Working robots in real fields with paying customers clear the prototype bar. Strategic relationships with Chipotle (supply chain credibility), MKC (ag cooperative), and ILS (cattle) demonstrate market pull from motivated incumbents. Revenue of $196K caps the execution score — product rollout is real but embryonic. $1.8M of $2.5M.
B
Sequoia · Endurance
Passes 5 of 6 — farmers aren't rich customers
Clarity of purpose is sharp: eliminate agricultural herbicides through autonomous mechanical weeding. Large market passes (herbicide market alone exceeds $30B; regenerative ag growing fast). Singular focus on broadacre row crops. Genuine pain killer — herbicide resistance is causing crop losses across the US corn belt. The contrarian bet (mechanical over chemical) is exactly the right kind of uncomfortable. Only miss: row-crop farmers operate on razor-thin margins, not the "rich customers" Sequoia's framework prizes.
B
Christensen · Innovation
Low-end disruption of the herbicide status quo
JTBD is clear: eliminate weeds without chemicals. Functional dimension is strong; emotional (doing right by the land and health) is present; social dimension (regenerative farming identity) is emerging. The CVP — chemical-free weeding-as-a-service — is differentiated but the profit formula is unproven at 7% gross margins. Key resources (robots, field crews) are in place; key processes (fleet operations, customer onboarding) are developing. Classic low-end disruption targeting farmers locked into expensive and harmful chemical dependency.
