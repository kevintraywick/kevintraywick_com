Tier 1 — Must Have
F1Revenue ModelPresent
Hardware sales of autonomous robots (Burro, Burro Verde, Burro Grande) plus the BOSS software platform for fleet management. Dealer channel for distribution alongside direct sales. Multiple SKUs at different price points serve different farm types and load requirements. Clear and standard for a robotics company at this stage.
F2Monthly Burn RateAbsent
Not publicly disclosed. With 44 employees, hardware manufacturing, and R&D on multiple product lines, monthly burn is likely $400K–$700K. The Series B of $24M provides estimated 18–36 months of runway at these levels, but without a stated number, an investor cannot verify the capital efficiency.
F3Use of FundsPartial
Series B stated to fund "broader product lines and expansion to additional agricultural sectors." New product development (Burro Grande) and new vertical entry (industrial, intermodal) are directionally clear, but no dollar allocation between headcount, R&D, manufacturing, and go-to-market is given.
F4Runway CalculationAbsent
Not stated. Cannot calculate without burn rate. At Series B stage, a professional investor would have this number — it's the absence from public materials that registers as absent here, not necessarily from the internal investor deck.
Tier 2 — Should Have
F53-Year P&L ProjectionAbsent
Not disclosed publicly. The path from 300 to 3,000+ units and the corresponding revenue, COGS, and margin evolution is the core financial story — and it's entirely private.
F6Unit Economics (CAC/LTV)Partial
Customer-side ROI is well documented: under 2 months payback, 48% productivity increase in table grapes. But the internal unit economics — what a Burro costs to manufacture, gross margin per unit, and LTV of a robot (given repair, software, and upgrade revenue) — are not disclosed.
F7Gross Margin TrajectoryAbsent
Not disclosed. Hardware companies at 300 units face very different margins than at 3,000 units. The BOM reduction curve, assembly automation, and software attach rate are all relevant — none are public.
F812-Month Cash Flow ProjectionAbsent
Not available publicly. Agricultural robotics has seasonality in deployment — understanding the cash flow peaks and troughs requires a monthly model that doesn't exist in public materials.
Tier 3 — Nice to Have
F9Cap Table SummaryPartial
Investor roster is known: Toyota Ventures, S2G Ventures, F-Prime Capital, Cibus Capital (Series A); Catalyst Investors, Translink Capital (Series B lead). Total raised: $44.5M. Exact ownership percentages, option pool, and SAFE/convertible note history not disclosed.
F10Revenue AssumptionsAbsent
No bottom-up model showing: units sold per quarter × ASP + software attach rate = projected revenue. The growth from 300 to 800 units (stated target) and the corresponding revenue implication is not modeled publicly.
F11Sensitivity AnalysisAbsent
Not present. Key variables to stress: robot ASP elasticity, manufacturing cost reduction curve, dealer channel vs. direct sales mix, and the impact of a major OEM entering the space with a competing product.
F12Comparable BenchmarksPartial
Labor market size ($1.2T outdoor) and specialty crop labor dependency (80-90% of farm workers) are cited, providing market context. No financial benchmarking against comparable hardware robotics companies (Boston Dynamics, Locus Robotics) or farm automation precedents.
